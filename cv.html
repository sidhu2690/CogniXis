About

I'm an undergraduate at IISER Mohali, pursuing an integrated BS-MS degree in Physics with a minor in Data Science. My academic journey is fueled by a deep curiosity about the universe, and I'm particularly drawn to high-energy physics and mathematics. Currently, I'm diving into the fascinating worlds of quantum mechanics and quantum machine learning, where I see immense potential for groundbreaking discoveries.

I've had the privilege of contributing to several exciting projects in high-energy physics. As part of the SWGO collaboration, I worked on optimizing detectors for a ground-based detector array, tackling real-world challenges in experimental design. I’ve also been involved in particle reconstruction for the CMS experiment, honing my skills in analyzing complex datasets. Beyond physics, I’m passionate about the mathematics underpinning deep learning. I’ve explored problems in AI interpretability, aiming to make machine learning models more transparent and reliable.

Right now, I’m venturing into quantum computing and quantum machine learning, blending my love for theoretical rigor with cutting-edge computational techniques. I thrive on collaborating with like-minded researchers and am always eager to learn new tools and methods. My goal is to push the boundaries of physics by combining traditional experimental approaches with modern computational innovations, contributing to our understanding of the universe’s fundamental building blocks.
